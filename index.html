Mr. Sujit Mustafa

Early Life and Education:

Mr. Sujit Mustafa was born into a lineage steeped in artistic tradition, the son of the late Abu Hena Mustafa Kamal, a renowned poet, singer, and composer. Hailing from Pabna, his musical journey was rooted in rigorous training from a young age. He honed his skills under the esteemed tutelage of Pandit Vinod Kumar at the Gandharva Mahavidyalaya in New Delhi and Pandit Amarnath of the Indore Gharana at Shriram Bharatia Kala Kendra. His academic pursuit in music was further solidified with a five-year certificate course at Chhayanaut and a diploma from the Bangladesh Shilpakala Academy, setting the stage for a distinguished career in classical music.

Career and Performances:

Mr. Mustafa’s professional career is marked by a diverse array of performances that have resonated both in Bangladesh and internationally. His ability to seamlessly blend classical, semi-classical, Nazrulgeeti, and modern Bangla songs has seen him grace many stages worldwide. A memorable moment in his career came in 1990 when he performed at the Delhi Siriford Auditorium for Pandit Ravi Shankar's 70th birthday. That same year, his talents were showcased at the Videshi Kalakar Uthsab in New Delhi, where he was selected among numerous international students. His music has touched audiences across various continents including the USA, UK, France, Australia, Japan, China, Nepal, and Sri Lanka, establishing him as a global ambassador of Bangladeshi classical music.

Media Appearances and Recognition:

Mr. Mustafa’s contributions to music have not gone unnoticed, earning him interviews and features with leading Bangladeshi and international media outlets, including the BBC, Radio Spectrum (UK), Ekushey Betar (Australia), and Brisbane Bangla Radio. His excellence in the field is recognized by his designation as a “Special” grade artist on Bangladesh TV and Bangladesh Betar, where he performs modern and Nazrul’s songs. This recognition is a testament to his skill, dedication, and the high regard in which he is held both at home and abroad.

Honoured with Nazrul Padak

Contributions and Editorial Work:

Beyond his performances, Mr. Mustafa is deeply committed to the dissemination and preservation of Bangladeshi culture. He has released several albums, with notable titles such as “Amar Ganer Prothom Choronkhani,” “Onek Brishty Jhore,” and “Dure Bohudure,” which have contributed significantly to the Bangla music landscape. He also serves as the editor of a monthly magazine dedicated to Kazi Nazrul Islam, Bangladesh’s national poet. Through this role, he not only honors the legacy of Nazrul but also fosters a deeper appreciation for the poet's work in the hearts of readers and listeners alike.
